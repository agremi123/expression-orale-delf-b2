Chapter 1

The Official Exam

Format, structure and scoring criteria

1.1 — The two parts

Part 1 — Extended Monologue 5–7 min
You receive a written document (article, interview, opinion piece). You must identify the key issue, present a clear structured opinion, and organize intro → development → conclusion. You choose 1 topic from 2.
Part 2 — Interaction / Debate 10–13 min
The examiner asks questions. You must defend your view, nuance and concede when appropriate, and react with relevance and politeness.

1.2 — Official scoring (25 points)

CriterionPtsWhat is assessed
Task — Monologue5Clear problem, logical plan, 2–3 illustrated arguments
Task — Interaction5Responsiveness, defense and nuance
Vocabulary5Range, relevance, few repetitions
Grammar5Complex structures, rare errors
Pronunciation5Intelligibility, natural intonation, fluency
Elimination rule At least 5/25 in each section and 50/100 overall — or you fail.
What examiners want Structured speech (not memorized), nuanced arguments with examples, natural communication, ability to react — not a perfect “correct” answer.

Chapter 2

Methods & Tools

Speech models, connectors and essential expressions

2.1 — Monologue model (3 steps)

1
Introduction (~1 min)

Cite source · State problem · Announce plan

« Cet article de [source] présente… On peut se demander si… J'aborderai d'abord X, puis Y. »

2
Development (6–8 min)

2–3 arguments with examples · Explicit transitions · Varied connectors

« D'une part… D'autre part… Cependant… Par exemple… »

Golden rule: Fully develop 2 arguments rather than listing 5 superficially.
3
Conclusion (~1 min)

Answer the problem · Summarize opinion · Open perspective

« En conclusion, pour ma part… Pour aller plus loin… »

2.2 — Debate strategies

StrategyExample phrase
Organized reply« D'abord… Ensuite… Enfin… »
Partial agreement« Je suis d'accord sur X, mais d'un autre côté… »
Nuancing« Vous avez un point, cependant… / Certes, mais… »
Buying time« C'est une bonne question, laissez-moi réfléchir… »
Clarifying« Pouvez-vous préciser votre question ? »

2.3 — Connectors by category

Introducing Premièrement, Pour commencer…
Adding De plus, En outre, Par ailleurs…
Opposing Cependant, Toutefois, En revanche…
Illustrating Par exemple, Ainsi, Notamment…
Conceding Certes, Il est vrai que, Bien que…
Concluding En conclusion, En somme, Au final…

Chapter 3

Corrected Sample Speeches

5 typical DELF B2 topics

Example 1 — Is school asking too much of parents?

Question: Should parents be more involved in schooling, or does this undermine teachers?

Plan: Intro (La Croix) → Arg+ co-education / Scandinavia → Arg− parent overload → Conclusion: cooperation, not obligation

Strengths: Clear intro, logical connectors, concession in debate.

Example 2 — Should remote work be encouraged?

Question: Beneficial or problematic for employees and society?

Plan: Survey intro → economic/time gains → loneliness/blurred boundaries → Moderate framework (1–2 days/week)

Example 3 — Does technology increase stress?

Plan: Benefits (info, remote work) → Drawbacks (overload, notifications) → Learn to disconnect

Example 4 — Social networks & freedom of expression

Plan: Voice for minorities → Fake news, filter bubbles → Media literacy education

Example 5 — Foreign accent & integration

Plan: Communication barriers → Accent as identity → Intercultural openness
