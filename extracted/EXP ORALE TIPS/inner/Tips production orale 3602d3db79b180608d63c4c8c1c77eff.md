# Tips production orale

### [**Part 1: Monologue (5 minutes)**](https://savoirx.ai/articles/delf-speaking-test-production-orale-guide#part-1-monologue-5-minutes)

Present your analysis and argue your position.

**Structure:**

1. **Introduction** (30-45 seconds)
    - Introduce the topic
    - State the main question/problem
    - Announce your position
2. **Development** (3-3.5 minutes)
    - Present arguments logically
    - Use examples and evidence
    - Address counterarguments
3. **Conclusion** (30-45 seconds)
    - Summarize your position
    - Offer perspective or nuance

**Key skills evaluated:**

- Clear, organized presentation
- Relevant, developed arguments
- Use of complex structures
- Appropriate vocabulary

1.  don't write everything down. If you just read off a paper, they will take a lot of points off. You should glance at your paper for reference and use it to organize your thoughts beforehand.
2. vocab, vocab, vocab. The DELF B2 exam is all about arguing your points and taking a stance on various issues. Vocab having to do with technology, ecology, work life, etc. ALSO, learn your transition words. ("Donc," "Cependant," "Ainsi," "Malgre," etc.) You'll need these and they will help you form better sentences. Make  flashcards. One of the best thing you can do is learn a ton of vocab and depending on what subject you get for the exam, jot a lot of it down during your writing time.
3.  tip is be prepared to mess up, and don't be afraid to correct yourself. Correcting yourself if you mess up a little is a great idea, which sounds counter-intuitive but it'll show the examiners that you can catch your own mistakes. Now, granted, don't stress over every little pronunciation error or weird word, but if you mess up a sentence or the grammar of a sentence and you instantly notice, don't be afraid to correct yourself. This can help you a lot.
4.  listen to French speakers *a lot*. Podcasts, radio shows, news outlets, movies. For one, this will help you on the audio comprehension part of the test (which is an absolute bitch, trust me) but it will also help you pick up on how French people speak, sentence structure, etc. Try to use French captions as well. Also, if you're looking to increase your French comprehension/speaking but you don't always want to listen to something impossibly difficult to understand, I recommend Inner French on YouTube. He has tons of great podcasts and intermediate French videos that have been super helpful for me.

## [**Evaluation Criteria (All Levels)**](https://savoirx.ai/articles/delf-speaking-test-production-orale-guide#evaluation-criteria-all-levels)

Examiners evaluate five main areas:

### [**1. Task Completion (Respect de la consigne)**](https://savoirx.ai/articles/delf-speaking-test-production-orale-guide#_1-task-completion-respect-de-la-consigne)

Did you understand and complete what was asked?

- Follow instructions correctly
- Address all parts of the task
- Stay on topic

### [**2. Sociolinguistic Competence**](https://savoirx.ai/articles/delf-speaking-test-production-orale-guide#_2-sociolinguistic-competence)

Is your register appropriate for the situation?

- Formal vs. informal language
- Politeness conventions
- Cultural appropriateness

### [**3. Vocabulary (Lexique)**](https://savoirx.ai/articles/delf-speaking-test-production-orale-guide#_3-vocabulary-lexique)

Is your vocabulary adequate for the topic?

- Range of vocabulary
- Precision of word choice
- Ability to paraphrase when needed

### [**4. Grammar (Morphosyntaxe)**](https://savoirx.ai/articles/delf-speaking-test-production-orale-guide#_4-grammar-morphosyntaxe)

Are your sentences correctly constructed?

- Appropriate tenses
- Agreement (gender, number)
- Sentence structure complexity (varies by level)

### [**5. Pronunciation and Fluency**](https://savoirx.ai/articles/delf-speaking-test-production-orale-guide#_5-pronunciation-and-fluency)

Can you be understood easily?

- Clear pronunciation
- Natural rhythm and intonation
- Fluency (not necessarily fast, but smooth)

## [**Common Mistakes to Avoid**](https://savoirx.ai/articles/delf-speaking-test-production-orale-guide#common-mistakes-to-avoid)

### [**At All Levels**](https://savoirx.ai/articles/delf-speaking-test-production-orale-guide#at-all-levels)

1. **One-word answers:** Always develop your responses
2. **Ignoring the examiner:** The test is a conversation, not a monologue
3. **Freezing on mistakes:** Self-correct briefly and continue
4. **Speaking too fast:** Clarity matters more than speed
5. **Forgetting politeness:** Always use appropriate greetings and forms

### [**At Higher Levels (B1-B2)**](https://savoirx.ai/articles/delf-speaking-test-production-orale-guide#at-higher-levels-b1-b2)

1. **No structure:** Organize your ideas before speaking
2. **No examples:** Abstract arguments need concrete support
3. **Ignoring counterarguments:** Acknowledge other perspectives
4. **Over-complicating:** Clear ideas beat complex-but-confused ones

## [**Preparation Strategies**](https://savoirx.ai/articles/delf-speaking-test-production-orale-guide#preparation-strategies)

### [**Build Speaking Confidence**](https://savoirx.ai/articles/delf-speaking-test-production-orale-guide#build-speaking-confidence)

1. **Practice out loud daily:** Even 5 minutes helps
2. **Record yourself:** Listen back to identify weaknesses
3. **Practice with a timer:** Get used to speaking for required durations
4. **Simulate the test:** Practice with a friend or tutor playing examiner

### [**Improve Fluency**](https://savoirx.ai/articles/delf-speaking-test-production-orale-guide#improve-fluency)

1. **Learn transition phrases:**
    - *D'abord, ensuite, enfin*
    - *D'un cote... de l'autre*
    - *Par exemple, notamment*
2. **Practice self-correction:**
    - *Pardon, je voulais dire...*
    - *Non, en fait...*
3. **Have "emergency" phrases:**
    - *Pouvez-vous repeter?*
    - *Je n'ai pas bien compris la question.*

### [**Topic Preparation**](https://savoirx.ai/articles/delf-speaking-test-production-orale-guide#topic-preparation)

For B1-B2, stay informed about current topics:

- Technology and daily life
- Environment and sustainability
- Work and education
- Social changes and trends
